Our Action Lines & Field Operations

The operational blueprint mapping out our tactical directives to safeguard our communities from physical, economic, and digital exploitation.

Deployment Operations

Countering Corporate Racism

Challenging modern economic exploitation by bringing historical truths directly to light. We audit and trace corporate wealth roots back to uncompensated systemic labor, pushing for institutional accountability and community reinvestment frameworks.

  • Ancestry Auditing: Unveiling historical assets built on exploitation.
  • Accountability Mandates: Pressuring legacy entities to engage in transparent corporate responsibility.
  • Economic Literacy: Dismantling systemic financial dependency through community-led awareness.

Ending Police Brutality

Deploying grassroots advocacy alongside technological defensive measures to safeguard urban spaces from systemic over-policing and physical violence. We focus on transparent community structures to balance the structural equilibrium.

  • Community Advocacy: Direct grassroots actions targeting institutional policies.
  • Neutral Monitoring: Deploying community safety nodes to create balanced observation parameters.
  • Rights Reclamation: Training neighborhoods in structural legal awareness and accountability protocols.

Surveillance & Profile Audits

Dismantling non-public, algorithmic database profiling systems. Law enforcement increasingly builds invisible threat and network profiles on regular citizens through closed AI data-fusion aggregators without public consent or transparency.

  • System Audits: Tracking data-fusion center parameters to protect biometric sovereignty.
  • Profile Mapping: Helping citizens unearth and audit hidden digital records.
  • Ecosystem Defense: Creating privacy frameworks to counteract algorithmic profiling.

Civic Tech Strategy: Exposing the Invisible Profiles

Because automated AI surveillance systems operate inside closed networks, regular citizens are completely unaware that threat metrics are being compiled on them. Thin Black and Brown Lines USA runs localized clinics helping community members leverage the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) to audit public profiles and hidden tracking architectures.

Vendor Contract Audits Targeting contracts and MOUs between local agencies and private analytics corporations to expose profiling software applications.
Access Log Tracking Demanding operational audit trails detailing how municipal systems transmit biometric parameters to regional data hubs.
Impact Assessments Forcing the public release of mandated algorithmic impact reviews to challenge biased profiling models.
